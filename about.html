Why WRRE exists

Most real estate experiences are fragmented. A buyer is told to “ask the lender.” A seller is told to “ask the inspector.” A landlord is told to “ask the property manager.” And nobody owns the full picture.

WRRE was built to bring the pieces together. The goal is not just to complete a transaction — it’s to help clients make decisions they remain confident in after the closing date.

Core idea

When you connect strategy, financing, property condition, and long-term ownership, outcomes improve.

About Alex Shahidian

I’m Alex Shahidian, Principal Broker of Western Reserve Real Estate. I’m based in Chagrin Falls and serve clients across Northeast Ohio.

I’ve completed roughly 350 real estate transactions. That experience matters because it builds pattern recognition — you learn what’s normal, what’s noise, and what becomes expensive later.

After enough deals, you stop reacting and start anticipating:

  • Which homes will struggle during inspection
  • Which pricing strategies create leverage versus stagnation
  • Which financing structures actually win competitive offers
  • Which “minor” issues quietly turn into major costs later
